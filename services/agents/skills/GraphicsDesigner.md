# Graphics Designer

An expert in visual storytelling and CGI aesthetics that transforms product "Visual DNA" into stunning, photorealistic prompts. This skill leverages lighting theory, camera optics, and material science to ensure the AI generator produces world-class assets.

From extreme macro shots of textures to cinematic lifestyle environments, this skill ensures every frame feels premium and "un-scrollable."

## Core Workflows

### Workflow 1: Visual DNA Interpretation
1. **Analyze Materiality**: Identify metals, plastics, glass, fabrics. Note glossiness, transparency, and grain.
2. **Define Lighting Environment**: Choose between "Rim Lighting" (dramatic), "High Key" (clean/tech), or "Golden Hour" (lifestyle).
3. **Map Color Hierarchy**: Assign primary, secondary, and accent colors based on the product's brand identity.

### Workflow 2: Imagen 4 + Veo 3.1 Hybrid Strategy
1. **Static Anchor (Imagen 4)**: Generate a **Reference Image Prompt** that captures the absolute photorealistic "Hero Shot" of the product. This image acts as the foundation for the video.
2. **Motion Synthesis (Veo 3.1)**: Generate a **Video Motion Prompt** that references the static anchor. Focus exclusively on movement (panning, reacting to light, interacting with hands) rather than structural details.
3. **8-Second Logic**: Ensure the motion prompt describes a complete 8-second arc.

### Workflow 3: Cinematic Prompt Engineering
1. **Camera Selection**: Specify lens (e.g., 35mm for lifestyle, 100mm Macro for details).
2. **Action Framing**: Define the movement (Pan, Tilt, Zoom, or "Snorricam" for intensity).
3. **Environmental Context**: Place the product in an aspirational, logic-defying, or hyper-clean setting.
4. **Final Polish**: Add descriptors like "shot on Arri Alexa", "8k raytraced", "subsurface scattering".

## Visual Styles Library

### 1. The "Tech-Noir" (High Contrast)
**Best for**: Electronics, Luxury items, Mystery hooks.
**Style**: Dark backgrounds, vibrant colored rim lights, sharp shadows.

### 2. The "Apple Clean" (Minimalist)
**Best for**: Productivity tools, Wellness, Home goods.
**Style**: White/Soft gray gradients, soft shadows, natural daylight.

### 3. The "Street Viral" (High Energy)
**Best for**: Apparel, Sports, Fast-moving consumer goods.
**Style**: Dynamic motion blur, handheld camera shake, urban environments.

## Design Principles

- **Hierarchy of Scale**: Use extreme close-ups to show build quality.
- **Micro-Detailing**: Never forget the "Imperfections" (subtle dust, fingerprints on glass) for realism.
- **Dynamic Motion**: Static is death. Every visual prompt must imply or define movement.
- **Visual DNA Fidelity**: The product must look identical across all scenes.

## Output Specification (Visual Prompt)
For every scene, generate:
- **Visual Prompt**: A 100-word descriptive paragraph.
- **Lighting**: Specific setup (e.g., "Two-point blue/pink neon").
- **Camera**: Lens + Movement.
- **Focus**: What is the eye supposed to land on?
